About Me

Collin Jacob — Portrait Placeholder

A third-year architecture student with a passion for contextual design, tectonic craft, and the intersection of built form with community.

Currently studying architecture, I approach each project as an inquiry into how space shapes human behaviour — and how human behaviour can, in turn, reshape space. My work spans intimate residential studies to large-scale urban interventions, with a consistent interest in materiality and structural honesty.

Outside of studio, I am drawn to architectural history, site-specific installation art, and the ways vernacular traditions continue to inform contemporary practice.
